# Sony E 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6 OSS — Corrected Optical Design Analysis

## 1. Patent Reference and Design Identification

**Patent:** US 2011/0273780 A1  
**Application Number:** 13/064,701  
**Filed:** April 11, 2011  
**Published:** November 10, 2011  
**Priority:** JP 2010-108665, May 10, 2010  
**Inventors:** Masaharu Hosoi, Hiroyuki Matsumoto, Masafumi Sueyoshi  
**Assignee:** Sony Corporation  
**Title:** Zoom Lens and Imaging Apparatus  
**Embodiment analyzed:** Numerical Example 1, Zoom lens 1, ¶0080-¶0097

The prescription transcribed here is Numerical Example 1 of Sony's 2011 zoom-lens patent. It is the only short-ratio embodiment in the filing whose focal-length range, format, element count, aspherical-surface count, stabilization arrangement, and rear-focus mechanism converge on the production Sony E 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6 OSS (SEL1855).

The production lens is an APS-C Sony E-mount zoom with a marketed 18-55mm focal-length range, f/3.5-5.6 maximum aperture range, 9 groups / 11 elements, 0.25 m minimum focus distance, 0.30x maximum magnification, 49 mm filter thread, Optical SteadyShot, and seven aperture blades. Sony also describes the optical system as using four aspherical surfaces. Numerical Example 1 gives 11 elements, 9 air-separated groups, focal lengths of 18.49 / 27.38 / 53.51 mm, F-numbers of 3.74 / 4.15 / 5.62, and four aspherical surfaces: S6, S16, S18, and S19. This is the strongest identification evidence.

A correction was made to the earlier analysis: the aspherical count should be stated as four aspherical **surfaces** on three physical aspherical elements, not merely as "3 aspherical lenses." That corrected wording matches both the patent table and Sony's own feature description.

The lens diagram and prose in the patent identify a five-group zoom with positive, negative, positive, negative, and positive group powers. Blur correction is performed by moving the positive lens nearest the object side in G3 perpendicular to the optical axis; focusing is performed by axial motion of G4. Both features match the architecture expected for Sony's first stabilized APS-C E-mount kit zoom.

## 2. Optical Architecture

The design is a five-group positive-negative-positive-negative-positive zoom:

| Group | Power | Computed group focal length | Elements | Primary role |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| G1 | + | +55.05 mm | L1 + L2 | Front collector and cemented achromat |
| G2 | - | -12.58 mm | L3, L4, L5 | Main variator |
| G3 | + | +16.11 mm | L6, stop, L7 + L8, L9 | Aperture-bearing compensator and OSS group |
| G4 | - | -20.75 mm | L10 | Single-element rear focus group |
| G5 | + | +53.68 mm | L11 | Final relay / field flattener |

All five lens groups move along the optical axis during zooming. Using the corrected back-focus solution described in Section 9, the group reference positions measured from the image plane are:

| Position | EFL | G1 | G2 | G3 | G4 | G5 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Wide | 18.4887 mm | 77.89 mm | 69.53 mm | 46.15 mm | 26.44 mm | 18.60 mm |
| Middle | 27.3753 mm | 81.36 mm | 68.08 mm | 50.60 mm | 30.33 mm | 23.05 mm |
| Tele | 53.5100 mm | 104.24 mm | 83.55 mm | 70.95 mm | 53.87 mm | 43.40 mm |

The front group extends strongly toward the object side during zooming, while the G1-G2 gap opens and the G2-G3 gap collapses. The moving G4 negative group provides both focus compensation during zoom and the focus-only motion used for near focusing. The stop is placed inside G3, between the stabilized L6 and the L7/L8 cemented doublet.

## 3. Element-by-Element Analysis

### L1 — Negative meniscus, convex to object (S1-S2)

`nd = 1.84666, νd = 23.78. Glass: S-TIH53 (OHARA). Standalone f = -158.88 mm.`

L1 is the flint half of the front cemented doublet. Both radii are positive, with the rear surface more strongly curved, producing a negative meniscus. Its very high index and low Abbe number supply the chromatic counter-power required by the positive front group while keeping the external curvatures moderate.

### L2 — Positive meniscus, convex to object (S2-S3)

`nd = 1.72916, νd = 54.66. Glass: S-LAL18 (OHARA). Standalone f = +40.66 mm.`

L2 is the positive crown half of the front doublet and provides most of G1's converging power. The cemented interface with L1 carries much of the achromatizing burden. The external rear surface is weak, which keeps the doublet's image-side curvature gentle while preserving positive group power.

### L3 — Negative meniscus, convex to object (S4-S5)

`nd = 1.910823, νd = 35.25. Glass: S-NBH53 (OHARA). Standalone f = -12.85 mm.`

L3 is the leading and strongest diverging element in the variator group. The patent prose explicitly describes it as a negative meniscus whose convex surface faces the object side. Its high index permits strong negative power without pushing the front surface to an extreme curvature.

### L4 — Biconcave negative, aspherical front surface (S6-S7)

`nd = 1.851348, νd = 40.10. Glass: 851401 high-index lanthanum-flint-class moldable glass (M-TAFD305 code match). Standalone f = -18.58 mm.`

L4 adds negative power inside G2 and places an aspherical surface on S6, where wide-angle off-axis rays are still high. This is one of the design's principal wide-end coma, astigmatism, and distortion-control surfaces.

The earlier analysis identified this glass as OHARA L-LAH85. That identification has been withdrawn. The patent's nd/νd pair, 1.851348 / 40.10, does not match the publicly verified OHARA L-LAH85V listing. Sweep 2 keeps the L-LAH85 attribution out of the data while allowing the 851401 code to resolve through the public HOYA M-TAFD305 row.

### L5 — Biconvex positive (S8-S9)

`nd = 1.92286, νd = 20.88. Glass: S-NPH2 (OHARA). Standalone f = +20.84 mm.`

L5 is a strong positive element placed at the rear of the negative variator group. Its very low Abbe number gives it a chromatic role that is opposite to a conventional crown in a positive group; here it helps balance the chromatic behavior of the net-negative variator assembly.

### L6 — Positive meniscus, convex to object; OSS element (S10-S11)

`nd = 1.61800, νd = 63.39. Glass: S-PHM52-class OHARA phosphate crown; patent νd = 63.39. Standalone f = +38.54 mm.`

L6 is the single blur-correction lens. It is moved perpendicular to the optical axis for image stabilization while the rest of the optical system remains fixed for the stabilization event. Its relatively high Abbe number is appropriate for a lens that is deliberately decentered during operation, because decentering a highly dispersive element would increase lateral color sensitivity.

### Aperture stop (S12)

The stop lies in the air space between L6 and L7. A constant stop semi-diameter of 4.862 mm reproduces the patent F-number sequence within rounding: f/3.74 at the wide end, about f/4.18 at the middle position, and about f/5.64 at the telephoto end. The middle and telephoto discrepancies are consistent with rounded patent F-number entries.

### L7 — Biconvex positive (S13-S14)

`nd = 1.48749, νd = 70.45. Glass: S-FSL5 (OHARA). Standalone f = +17.81 mm.`

L7 is the low-dispersion positive element immediately behind the stop. Because marginal-ray height is high near the stop, L7 is a major axial-color contributor. Its pairing with L8 forms the second chromatic-correction zone of the design.

### L8 — Negative meniscus, concave to object (S14-S15)

`nd = 1.805181, νd = 25.46. Glass: S-TIH6 (OHARA). Standalone f = -27.78 mm.`

L8 is the flint partner cemented to L7. The wide Abbe split across the cemented interface gives this doublet strong primary chromatic correction behind the aperture stop.

### L9 — Positive meniscus, concave to object; aspherical front surface (S16-S17)

`nd = 1.72903, νd = 54.04. Glass: 729540 lanthanum-crown-class moldable glass (M-TAC80 code match). Standalone f = +32.62 mm.`

L9 is the final element of G3 and carries the largest A4 coefficient in the prescription. It is the main post-stop aspherical field-correction element. The 729540 code now resolves to HOYA M-TAC80 coefficients; this is used as a code-backed dispersion representative rather than a production-supplier assertion.

### L10 — Negative meniscus, both surfaces aspherical; focus element (S18-S19)

`nd = 1.851348, νd = 40.10. Glass: 851401 high-index lanthanum-flint-class moldable glass (M-TAFD305 code match). Standalone f = -20.75 mm.`

L10 is the whole fourth group and the only moving group used for focusing. Both surfaces are aspherical. Its negative power makes the focus sensitivity high enough to reach the production 0.25 m minimum focus distance with only a few millimeters of paraxial group motion at the telephoto end.

The earlier analysis assigned the same OHARA L-LAH85 identity used for L4. That assignment is not retained. The glass is treated as a moldable high-index lanthanum-flint-class material because it carries two aspherical surfaces; the public dispersion representative is the 851401 HOYA M-TAFD305 code match.

### L11 — Plano-convex positive (S20-S21)

`nd = 1.58144, νd = 40.89. Glass: E-FL5 (HOYA). Standalone f = +53.68 mm.`

L11 is the final relay and field-flattening element. The patent prose calls the fifth group a positive meniscus, but Table 1 gives S21 as INF, making the numerical prescription plano-convex. The numerical prescription is controlling here.

The major correction in this section is the treatment of the 1.851348 / 40.10 material used for L4 and L10. The previous draft used a named OHARA moldable-glass attribution. The chromatic strategy remains clear even with this uncertainty. L9 and L10 are aspherical correction elements rather than uniquely identifiable catalog-glass anchors.

## 5. Focus Mechanism

The patent states that focusing is performed by moving the fourth lens group G4 along the optical axis. In Numerical Example 1, G4 consists only of L10. This is therefore a single-element rear-focus system.

Sony's published production specification gives a 0.25 m minimum focus distance and 0.30x maximum magnification. Because the patent gives only the infinity-focus zoom prescription, the data file uses a paraxial refocus solve to estimate the close-focus motion while conserving the D17 + D19 spacing around G4. The result is:

| Position | D17 infinity | D19 infinity | D17 at 0.25 m | D19 at 0.25 m | Paraxial magnification |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Wide | 5.510 mm | 6.841 mm | 6.221 mm | 6.130 mm | 0.097x |
| Middle | 6.071 mm | 6.280 mm | 7.397 mm | 4.954 mm | 0.141x |
| Tele | 2.887 mm | 9.465 mm | 5.848 mm | 6.504 mm | 0.298x |

The telephoto result agrees with Sony's 0.30x maximum-magnification specification to the accuracy expected from a paraxial calculation.

## 6. Aspherical Surfaces

The patent uses the standard even-order aspherical sag equation:

$$
x(y)=\frac{c y^2}{1+\sqrt{1-(1+K)c^2y^2}}+A_4y^4+A_6y^6+A_8y^8+A_{10}y^{10}
$$

Here `K = 0` is the spherical-base convention, not a shifted Japanese κ convention. All four aspherical surfaces in Numerical Example 1 have K = 0.

| Surface | Element | K | A4 | A6 | A8 | A10 |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| S6 | L4 front | 0 | -7.49645e-6 | -7.70344e-8 | +6.67223e-10 | -1.38051e-11 |
| S16 | L9 front | 0 | -1.24878e-4 | +1.87829e-7 | -1.51018e-9 | -1.96142e-11 |
| S18 | L10 front | 0 | -5.85209e-6 | -8.97199e-7 | +1.91120e-8 | -1.26629e-10 |
| S19 | L10 rear | 0 | -3.28062e-5 | -6.60272e-7 | +1.86921e-8 | -1.24801e-10 |

S6 moderates the wide-end aberration contribution of the negative variator. S16 is the strongest aspherical correction surface by A4 magnitude and is therefore the principal field-correction asphere in G3. S18 and S19 place aberration correction directly on the rear-focus element, reducing focus-shift aberration changes as G4 moves.

## 7. Image Stabilization

The patent's central stabilization feature is that only L6, the positive lens closest to the object side in G3, is moved perpendicular to the optical axis for blur correction. The background section contrasts this with prior arrangements that moved an entire group or a multiple-lens subgroup. The purpose is to reduce the moving mass of the stabilization group while keeping decenter-induced aberrations bounded by the magnification conditions.

L6 lies in front of the aperture stop. The patent explains this placement as mechanically useful because the blur-correction lens can be placed closest to the object side of the cam cylinder, leaving space for the correction mechanism.

## 8. Conditional Expressions

The patent gives three conditional expressions for the stabilized, rear-focus zoom architecture.

| Expression | Patent value for Zoom lens 1 | Verification / note |
|---|---:|---|
| `(1 - βkFW) · βkRW` | 0.94 | Table 13 gives βkFW = 103.16 and βkRW = -0.01; rounded values alone give 1.02, so the table value is evidently computed from unrounded magnifications. |
| `fk / f3` | 2.39 | Direct paraxial verification gives `fk = 38.538 mm`, `f3 = 16.1095 mm`, ratio 2.392. |
| `(1 - βG4t)^2 · (βG5t)^2` | 4.55 | Table 13 gives βG4t = 11.06 and βG5t = 0.21; rounded values give about 4.46. |

All three expressions are satisfied.

## 10. Data-File Implementation Notes

The `.data.ts` file follows the project zoom-lens convention: each zoom-variable spacing is represented as an array of `[infinity, close]` pairs at the three zoom positions. Pure zoom gaps use equal infinity and close values. D17 and D19 receive close-focus values from the paraxial refocus solution. D21 is treated as back focus and uses the corrected BFD sequence.

The patent does not provide semi-diameters. The data file therefore uses conservative inferred semi-diameters. The estimates were constrained by marginal and off-axis ray clearance at the viewer's default off-axis field fraction, element edge thickness, element front/rear SD ratio, `sd/|R| < 0.90`, and signed cross-gap sag intrusion. These values are suitable for LensVisualizer rendering and first-order analysis, but they should not be read as Sony-published mechanical clear apertures.

No sensor cover glass, filters, or mechanical barrel components are included.
