# Sigma 14-24mm F2.8 DG HSM | Art — Optical Analysis

## Patent Reference and Design Identification

**Patent:** JP 2018-189733 A (特開2018-189733)
**Application Number:** 特願2017-90338 (JP 2017-090338)
**Filed:** 2017-04-28 (平成29年4月28日)
**Published:** 2018-11-29 (平成30年11月29日)
**Inventor:** Ryo Shioda (塩田 了)
**Applicant:** Sigma Corporation (株式会社シグマ)
**Title:** 広角レンズ系 (Wide-Angle Lens System)
**Classification:** G02B 15/20, G02B 13/18
**Embodiment analyzed:** Numerical Example 1 (数値実施例1)

Numerical Example 1 is a three-group full-frame ultra-wide zoom design covering 14.50-23.15 mm at F2.93 with a 21.63 mm image height. The prescription is the best match to the production **Sigma 14-24mm F2.8 DG HSM | Art** for DSLR mounts.

The identification rests on convergent evidence rather than a single rounded specification:

1. The prescription resolves to 17 glass elements in 11 air-separated groups, matching Sigma's published 17/11 construction.
2. The patent design contains three nd 1.43700 / νd 95.10 fluorophosphate elements and three additional high-Abbe low-dispersion elements, matching Sigma's published three FLD and three SLD elements.
3. Five aspherical surfaces occur on three elements: L1, L3, and L17. Sigma's production description likewise gives three aspherical elements and specifically identifies the first element as a large-diameter asphere.
4. The patent image height is 21.63 mm and the full field is 112.80° at the wide end, making the design a 135-format ultra-wide zoom.
5. The design focal range, 14.50-23.15 mm, and design aperture, F2.93, round naturally to the marketed 14-24 mm F2.8 specification.
6. The wide-end air-equivalent back focal distance is 38.85 mm, consistent with a long-back-focus SLR design rather than Sigma's later DG DN mirrorless optical formula.

## Optical Architecture

Example 1 is a negative-lead retrofocus ultra-wide zoom. The patent divides it into a negative first lens group **G1**, followed by a positive rear group **GR**. GR is subdivided into a positive **G2** and a positive **G3**, with the aperture stop placed between G2 and G3 and moving with G3 during zooming (¶0054). The power sequence is therefore **negative - positive - positive**.

The verified paraxial group powers are:

| Group | Patent surfaces | Elements | Computed f (mm) | Patent f (mm) | Role |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| G1 | 1-11 | L1-L6 | -25.291 | -25.2907 | Negative front group; field angle, distortion, and long-BF generation |
| G2 | 12-17 | L7-L10 | +72.536 | +72.5361 | Inner-focus relay group |
| G3 | 18-29 | L11-L17 | +55.073 | +55.0724 | Stop-bearing positive relay and field-finishing group |

From wide to tele, d11 decreases from 29.2621 mm to 2.3000 mm, while d17 increases from 8.4844 mm to 9.7754 mm (¶0059). The total track shortens from 169.4000 mm to 157.9000 mm, so the optical prescription is a collapsing zoom rather than an extending zoom.

The architectural distinction is the split front asphere strategy. G1 uses a large entrance asphere SA1 on L1 and a second aspherical element L3 carrying SA2 plus a rear companion asphere. The patent states that SA1 corrects the negative distortion generated by the spherical negative front group, while SA2 corrects residual distortion and astigmatism undulation that SA1 alone cannot remove (¶0023, ¶0027). This division avoids forcing all distortion correction onto one very large, high-departure asphere.

## Element-by-Element Analysis

Standalone focal lengths below are thick-lens in-air values computed directly from the element radii, thicknesses, and d-line indices. They are not in-situ powers inside cemented groups.

### G1 - Negative Front Group

#### L1 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object; front asphere

nd = 1.69350, νd = 53.18, θgF = 0.5482. Glass: **OHARA L-LAL13** low-Tg molded-asphere glass. f = -65.1 mm.

L1 is the signature entrance element. Both radii are positive, so the element is an object-convex negative meniscus. Its front surface is SA1 (¶0056). The patent requires SA1 to have a form in which positive power strengthens, or negative power weakens, toward the edge (¶0023). With a positive A4 term and the verified local curvature trend, surface 1 satisfies that direction.

The physical production context matters here: Sigma describes the first element as a large-diameter aspherical element, and its own technology text links the lens to molded glass aspheres up to φ80 mm. The data file semi-diameter is not intended to reproduce that mechanical outside diameter; it is a renderer-safe optical clear aperture.

#### L2 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object; LS1

nd = 2.00100, νd = 29.13, θgF = 0.5994. Glass: **HOYA TAFD55/TAFD55-W class**, code 001/291. f = -61.8 mm.

This is LS1 in the patent (¶0056), the high-index negative lens constrained by condition (1), ndLS1 > 1.91. It is the highest-index element in the design. Its role is not merely to add negative power; the high index allows the required power with less severe curvature, helping limit Petzval and astigmatism penalties near SA1 and SA2 (¶0026).

#### L3 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object; both faces aspheric

nd = 1.58913, νd = 61.25, θgF = 0.5373. Glass: **HOYA M-BACD5N/BACD5 class**, code 589/613. f = -100.9 mm.

L3 is the second molded aspherical element in the front group. The object-side face is SA2; both surfaces 5 and 6 are aspheric (¶0055-0056). SA2 is positioned far enough behind the entrance asphere that the off-axis bundle has separated in height, but not so far back that its effect on distortion is lost. This is the geometric purpose of condition (2), DSA2/DG1 < 0.525 (¶0027).

#### L4 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object; SLD candidate

nd = 1.55032, νd = 75.50, θgF = 0.5400. Glass: **HOYA FCD705**, code 550/755. f = -182.3 mm.

L4 is weakly powered but chromatically important. Its high Abbe number and positive anomalous partial dispersion help raise the mean Δθ of the negative lenses in G1. That mean value is the quantity used by condition (4) to suppress residual g-line lateral color after ordinary C/F achromatization (¶0035).

#### L5 - Biconcave negative; FLD

nd = 1.43700, νd = 95.10, θgF = 0.5334. Glass: **HOYA FCD100 / S-FPL53-class fluorophosphate**. f = -56.6 mm.

L5 is the negative member of the G1 cemented doublet L5+L6 and one of the three FLD-class elements. Its high anomalous partial dispersion, Δθ = +0.0564 by the patent's formula, is the strongest contributor to condition (4) in the front group.

#### L6 - Biconvex positive; LS2

nd = 1.90043, νd = 37.37, θgF = 0.5772. Glass: **HOYA TAFD37A / high-index lanthanum flint class**, code 900/374. f = +38.8 mm.

L6 is LS2 in the patent (¶0056), the positive lens constrained by condition (3), ndLS2 > 1.73. It is cemented to L5 and supplies the positive correction that the all-negative front menisci cannot supply by themselves, especially for higher-order distortion and chromatic balance (¶0023, ¶0028).

### G2 - Inner-Focusing Group

#### L7 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.92286, νd = 20.88, θgF = 0.6388. Glass: **SCHOTT N-SF66 / HOYA E-FDS1-W class**, code 923/209. f = -38.2 mm.

L7 is the negative flint member of the first G2 cemented doublet. The patent's νd = 20.88 and 923/209 six-digit code match the N-SF66/E-FDS1 class rather than the nearby OHARA S-NPH2 glass. The element's high dispersion makes it the color-balancing flint partner for L8 in the moving focusing group.

#### L8 - Positive Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.72047, νd = 34.71, θgF = 0.5834. Glass: dense flint class, code 720/347. f = +33.9 mm.

L8 is cemented behind L7. The L7/L8 pair is a compact flint-forward achromat that begins reconverging the beam after the negative front group. Because G2 moves during focusing (¶0057), this pair must hold axial and lateral color tolerably as its air gaps to G1 and G3 change.

#### L9 - Biconvex positive

nd = 1.85478, νd = 24.80, θgF = 0.6122. Glass: high-index high-dispersion class, code 855/248. f = +22.8 mm.

L9 is the stronger positive lens in G2 and carries most of the group's converging power. It is cemented to L10, producing a positive achromat rather than a simple singlet relay.

#### L10 - Biconcave negative

nd = 1.87071, νd = 40.73, θgF = 0.5682. Glass: **HOYA TAFD32 / high-index lanthanum class**, code 871/407. f = -28.4 mm.

L10 is the negative partner of the L9/L10 doublet. The doublet remains net positive, but the negative component moderates the power and chromatic behavior of L9.

### G3 - Stop-Bearing Positive Relay

The aperture stop is surface 18 and translates with G3 during zooming (¶0054).

#### L11 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.83481, νd = 42.72, θgF = 0.5648. Glass: **HOYA TAFD5G / OHARA S-LAH55V-class equivalent**. f = -43.1 mm.

L11 is the first element after the stop and the front negative member of the L11+L12+L13 cemented triplet. Its position immediately behind the stop makes it important for spherical aberration and coma balancing in the converging relay.

#### L12 - Biconvex positive; FLD

nd = 1.43700, νd = 95.10, θgF = 0.5334. Glass: **HOYA FCD100 / S-FPL53-class fluorophosphate**. f = +27.3 mm.

L12 is the central positive core of the triplet and the second FLD-class element. It sits behind the stop, where the axial bundle is large, making it efficient for axial secondary-spectrum correction. It is one of the main contributors to condition (5), μΔθ2 > 0.010 (¶0036).

#### L13 - Negative Meniscus, convex to image

nd = 1.88300, νd = 40.80, θgF = 0.5654. Glass: **HOYA TAFD30 / OHARA S-LAH58-class equivalent**. f = -44.1 mm.

L13 completes the post-stop triplet. Its negative meniscus form and high index help confine the strong power of L12 while maintaining a controlled exit from the triplet.

#### L14 - Biconvex positive; FLD

nd = 1.43700, νd = 95.10, θgF = 0.5334. Glass: **HOYA FCD100 / S-FPL53-class fluorophosphate**. f = +40.3 mm.

L14 is a standalone FLD-class positive element. Together with L12 and L16, it drives the positive post-stop anomalous-dispersion average used in condition (5), correcting the relay's g-line axial color (¶0036).

#### L15 - Negative Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.88300, νd = 40.80, θgF = 0.5654. Glass: **HOYA TAFD30 / OHARA S-LAH58-class equivalent**. f = -41.3 mm.

L15 is the high-index negative member of the L15+L16 cemented doublet. The L15/L16 interface is one of the rear cemented surfaces used to balance coma and spherical aberration in the manner described by claim 6 and ¶0048-0049.

#### L16 - Positive Meniscus, convex to object; SLD candidate

nd = 1.49700, νd = 81.61, θgF = 0.5388. Glass: **HOYA FCD1 / SCHOTT N-PK52A / S-FPL51-equivalent**. f = +55.9 mm.

L16 is a low-dispersion positive element behind the stop. Its catalog numbers are a direct match to HOYA's FCD1 code 497/816 and to SCHOTT N-PK52A; it should not be described as exact OHARA S-FPL51, though it is in that equivalence class. Its Δθ = +0.0375 makes it a significant participant in condition (5).

#### L17 - Positive Meniscus, convex to image; both faces aspheric

nd = 1.55332, νd = 71.68, θgF = 0.5402. Glass: moldable low-dispersion class, code 553/717. f = +131.8 mm.

L17 is the final element and carries aspherical surfaces 28 and 29. The patent favors an asphere on the most image-side lens of GR because the off-axis bundle is relatively large there while the on-axis bundle is small; the asphere can therefore act on coma and astigmatism with limited disturbance to spherical aberration (¶0050-0051).

## Focus Mechanism

The patent specifies inner focusing by moving the whole G2 block, L7-L10, toward the image plane from infinity to close focus (¶0057). G1 and G3 remain fixed during focusing; the stop is part of G3 and does not participate in focusing. This is the mass-reduction compromise described in ¶0039-0040: moving G1 would enlarge the front group, while moving all of GR would make the focusing unit too heavy.

The numerical table for Example 1 gives only infinity-focus spacings at the three zoom positions. It does not provide close-focus d11/d17/BF values for the zoom example. The `.data.ts` file therefore records zoom-only variable spacings. The production lens's official minimum focusing distance is 0.26 m at the 24 mm end with 1:5.4 maximum magnification; those manufacturer specifications are retained as catalog metadata, not used to invent an undocumented focus trace.

## Aspherical Surfaces

The patent's asphere form (¶0120) is:

$$z = \frac{(1/r)y^2}{1 + \sqrt{1 - (1+K)(y/r)^2}} + \sum_{i=3}^{16} A_i y^i$$

Here K is the standard conic constant: K = 0 is a spherical base conic. All Example 1 aspheres have K = 0. Surfaces 5 and 6 include odd polynomial terms; surfaces 1, 28, and 29 are even-only.

| Surface | Element face | Patent role | Polynomial content | Data-file treatment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | L1 object face | SA1 | even A4-A12 | patent coefficients retained |
| 5 | L3 object face | SA2 | odd + even A3-A16 | even-order least-squares refit |
| 6 | L3 image face | companion to SA2 | odd + even A3-A16 | even-order least-squares refit |
| 28 | L17 object face | rear field correction | even A4-A16 | patent coefficients retained |
| 29 | L17 image face | rear field correction | even A4-A16 | patent coefficients retained |

The unsupported odd-order terms on surfaces 5 and 6 are not silently discarded. They are fitted into an even-order A4-A16 basis over the 17.5 mm semi-diameter used in the data file. The maximum residuals are 1.59 μm on surface 5 and 1.64 μm on surface 6, with RMS residuals of 0.645 μm and 0.665 μm respectively. This approximation is explicitly a renderer compatibility step; the patent prescription remains the analytical source.

The Petzval value is a surface-by-surface sum, Σφ/(n n′), not an element-level thin-lens approximation. The positive sum corresponds to a Petzval radius of about 218 mm in magnitude.

Semi-diameters were also checked for element edge thickness, front/rear SD ratios, rim slope, and cross-gap sag intrusion. The tightest retained margins are L8 edge thickness at about 0.53 mm and the surface 27-28A air gap intrusion at about 2.65 mm against a 2.71 mm 90%-gap limit.
