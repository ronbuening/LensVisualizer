Fujifilm's published specification gives 16 elements in 11 groups, four aspherical elements, three ED elements, one Super ED element, a constant F2.8 aperture, 16–55 mm focal range, 0.30 m minimum focusing distance, 0.21× maximum magnification, eleven aperture blades, 410 g mass, and a 72 mm filter thread. Example 1 gives f = 16.50 / 33.99 / 53.40 mm and FNo. = 2.88 / 2.88 / 2.89, uses four double-sided aspherical elements, and contains one νd ≈ 95 fluorite-class crown plus three νd ≈ 81.6 fluorophosphate crowns. The patent filing and priority dates also sit immediately before the production announcement window.

The match is strong but not unique in the mathematical sense. Other worked examples in the same patent share the same broad product-class signature. Example 1 is the lead embodiment illustrated in FIG. 1 and the only embodiment transcribed here.

## Optical Architecture

The lens is a positive-lead five-group zoom with the refractive-power sequence **positive – negative – positive – negative – positive**. In patent notation the groups are G1, G2, G3, G4, and G5/GE. The intermediate group GM consists of G3 and G4, while the final group GE is G5. The aperture stop is immediately before G3.

Two group counts are both correct. The patent's five groups are zoom/focus kinematic units. Fujifilm's published 11 groups are the air-separated optical clusters: L11–L12, L13, L21, L22–L23, L24, L31, L32–L33, L34–L35, L36, L41–L42, and L51.

G1 is a positive front collector made from a near-afocal cemented doublet followed by a positive meniscus. G2 is the negative variator and carries the strongest single negative element in the system. G3 is the principal positive converging group and is the Mp group used in the conditional expressions. G4 is a negative cemented focus doublet. G5 is a fixed positive rear aspheric element.

During zooming, G1 through G4 move while G5 remains fixed to the image plane. The four variable gaps at infinity focus are:

| Gap | Location | Wide | Middle | Tele |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| DD[5] | G1 to G2 | 0.80 | 19.17 | 30.67 |
| DD[12] | G2 to G3 | 20.91 | 5.28 | 1.00 |
| DD[23] | G3 to G4 | 1.71 | 4.15 | 3.31 |
| DD[26] | G4 to G5 | 7.33 | 13.09 | 22.84 |

DD[5] opens monotonically and DD[12] closes monotonically from wide to tele. DD[23] reverses between the middle and tele positions, so G3/G4 spacing is not a simple one-direction sweep. Back focus remains 17.92 mm in Table 2.

## Element-by-Element Analysis

### L11 — Negative Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.84666, νd = 23.79. Glass: FDS90 / S-TIH53W class, dense short flint. f = -189.7 mm.

L11 is the negative front member of the cemented L11–L12 entrance doublet. Its high dispersion and elevated partial-dispersion ratio make it the flint partner for the following ED crown. In air it is a weak negative meniscus, but the cemented pair is nearly afocal, so its primary role is chromatic correction at large entrance ray heights rather than first-order power.

### L12 — Positive Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.49700, νd = 81.55. Glass: FCD1B / S-FPL51 class, ED fluorophosphate. f = 193.2 mm.

L12 supplies the low-dispersion positive half of the front cemented doublet. The position of this element in Fujifilm's ED count is inferred from the patent glass data; Fujifilm publishes the number of ED/Super ED elements but not their slots.

### L13 — Positive Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.72915, νd = 54.64. Glass: TAC8 / S-LAL18 class, lanthanum crown. f = 94.8 mm.

L13 carries most of G1's positive power. Its high-index crown glass keeps the curvatures moderate for a front collector in a compact F2.8 standard zoom.

### L21 — Negative Meniscus, convex to object, double-sided asphere

nd = 1.80780, νd = 40.86. Glass: MC-NBFD130 / L-LAH53 class, moldable lanthanum dense flint. f = -18.4 mm.

L21 is the principal negative element of the variator. Both surfaces are aspherical, placing strong shape correction at the part of the zoom that produces the largest off-axis aberration changes. This is also one of the elements for which a molded-glass family is more plausible than a conventional polished catalog glass.

### L22 — Biconcave Negative

nd = 1.51741, νd = 52.16. Glass: E-CF6 / S-NSL36 class, light flint. f = -21.8 mm.

L22 is the negative half of G2's cemented doublet. Its biconcave form and light-flint dispersion let the L22–L23 pair correct chromatic error within the negative variator without making the whole pair strongly negative.

### L23 — Biconvex Positive

nd = 1.85027, νd = 32.27. Glass: S-LAH71 / N-LASF9 class, lanthanum dense flint. f = 17.0 mm.

L23 is the positive partner of L22. The cemented pair is weakly positive in isolation, embedded within a negative group. This lets G2 retain net negative power while controlling chromatic and spherical aberration through the zoom range.

### L24 — Negative Meniscus, convex to image

nd = 1.49700, νd = 81.61. Glass: FCD1 / S-FPL51 class, ED fluorophosphate. f = -55.9 mm.

L24 is the rear element of G2 and the second inferred ED element. Its low index and high Abbe number reduce chromatic coma and lateral color contributions from the variator.

### L31 — Biconvex Positive, double-sided asphere

nd = 1.68863, νd = 31.20. Glass: M-FD80 / L-TIM28 class, moldable flint. f = 28.3 mm.

L31 sits immediately behind the aperture stop. Its double-sided asphere is positioned where the on-axis marginal bundle is most directly shaped, making it a principal spherical-aberration and coma corrector at full aperture.

### L32 — Negative Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.85451, νd = 25.15. Glass: NBFD25 / S-NBH56 class, high-θgF dense flint. f = -37.9 mm.

L32 is the negative flint in the object-side G3 cemented doublet. Paired with the following Super ED crown, it forms the system's strongest secondary-spectrum corrector.

### L33 — Positive Meniscus, convex to object

nd = 1.43700, νd = 95.12. Glass: FCD100 / S-FPL53 class, Super ED fluorophosphate. f = 59.3 mm.

L33 is the Super ED element inferred from the νd ≈ 95 glass in Table 1. Its partial-dispersion ratio is far from the normal line, giving the L32–L33 doublet leverage over secondary spectrum rather than merely primary axial color.

### L34 — Plano-concave Negative

nd = 1.85451, νd = 25.15. Glass: NBFD25 / S-NBH56 class, high-θgF dense flint. f = -25.7 mm.

L34 begins the second cemented chromatic pair in G3. Its object surface is flat and its image-side surface is concave, making it a compact negative element ahead of the ED crown L35.

### L35 — Biconvex Positive

nd = 1.49700, νd = 81.61. Glass: FCD1 / S-FPL51 class, ED fluorophosphate. f = 37.9 mm.

L35 is the positive ED member of the L34–L35 doublet and the third inferred ED element. Together with the L32–L33 pair, it distributes axial-color correction across G3 rather than relying on a single large ED element.

### L36 — Biconvex Positive, double-sided asphere

nd = 1.61884, νd = 63.56. Glass: PCD4 / S-PHM52 class, anomalous phosphate crown. f = 20.6 mm.

L36 is a strong final positive element in G3 and closes the group with a double-sided asphere. It is not the strongest positive element in the full lens by standalone focal length; that distinction belongs to L23, the positive half of the G2 cemented doublet. It is not counted as one of Fujifilm's ED elements here, but its anomalous partial dispersion still helps trim residual color.

### L41 — Biconvex Positive

nd = 1.98613, νd = 16.48. Glass: FDS16-W class, extreme-index short flint. f = 65.2 mm.

L41 is the positive half of the moving focus doublet. Its very high refractive index allows useful power and color leverage with modest curvature.

### L42 — Biconcave Negative

nd = 1.83400, νd = 37.21. Glass: NBFD10 / S-LAH60V class, lanthanum dense flint. f = -22.0 mm.

L42 is the negative half of G4. The L41–L42 cemented doublet has net negative power, matching the patent's group-power sequence and giving the internal-focus unit enough power to focus with relatively small axial travel.

### L51 — Positive Meniscus, convex to image, double-sided asphere

nd = 1.51625, νd = 64.05. Glass: BSC7 / BK7-class moldable crown. f = 98.3 mm.

L51 is the only element in G5/GE. It is fixed during zooming and focusing in Example 1 and uses two aspherical faces to control exit-ray geometry, field curvature, and distortion at the rear of the system.

## Focus Mechanism

The patent identifies G4 as the focus group and states that it moves toward the image side when focusing from infinity to the nearest object. G4 is a single cemented L41–L42 doublet. This is consistent with the production lens's internal linear-motor focusing, but the patent does not publish finite-conjugate spacing tables.

For the data file, close-focus values were therefore fitted paraxially to Fujifilm's 0.30 m minimum-focus specification while preserving total track length by increasing DD[23] and decreasing DD[26] by the same amount. The telephoto fit gives 0.213× paraxial magnification, close to Fujifilm's 0.21× maximum-magnification specification.

| Zoom | G4 image-side shift | DD[23] close | DD[26] close | Paraxial magnification |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Wide | +0.704 mm | 2.414 | 6.626 | 0.076× |
| Middle | +2.228 mm | 6.378 | 10.862 | 0.143× |
| Tele | +3.831 mm | 7.141 | 19.009 | 0.213× |

These close-focus values are viewer-model values, not patent-tabulated values.

## Aspherical Surfaces

Four elements are aspherical on both faces: L21 (surfaces 6 and 7), L31 (14 and 15), L36 (22 and 23), and L51 (27 and 28). The data file labels these surfaces 6A, 7A, 14A, 15A, 22A, 23A, 27A, and 28A.

The patent's sag expression uses KA in the radical as `sqrt(1 − KA·C²h²)`. In the standard LensVisualizer convention this corresponds to `K = KA − 1`. Every Example 1 asphere has KA = 1.0000000, so the standard conic constant is K = 0 and the base conic is spherical.

The patent includes odd polynomial powers A3, A5, A7, and A9 as well as even powers A4, A6, A8, and A10. LensVisualizer's current data format supports even-order polynomial terms only. The data file therefore uses even-order least-squares refits A4–A14 over the stored clear semi-diameters. ## Chromatic Correction Strategy

The design uses three chromatic-correction zones. First, the L11–L12 front doublet is nearly afocal but combines a dense short flint with an ED crown, correcting color where entrance ray heights are large. Second, G2 places an ED negative meniscus at L24 to reduce chromatic coma and lateral color from the variator. Third, G3 contains two cemented chromatic pairs: L32–L33 and L34–L35.

The L32–L33 pair is the most important secondary-spectrum pair. Its Abbe split is νd(L33) − νd(L32) = 69.97 and its partial-dispersion split is θgF(L33) − θgF(L32) = −0.07544. The L34–L35 pair gives νd(L35) − νd(L34) = 56.46 and θgF(L35) − θgF(L34) = −0.07144. These are exactly the quantities used in conditional expressions (21)–(26).

The result should be described as a strongly corrected achromat with explicit secondary-spectrum control in G3. The patent does not claim full apochromatic performance across all fields and focal lengths.

## Conditional Expressions

Four require caution: (2) depends on the open F-number as used by the patent, and (3), (4), and (11) depend on ft·tan(ωt). Using Table 2's telephoto half-field of 14.7° does not reproduce the patent's values for (3), (4), and (11); those three values imply an effective full telephoto field of about 27.9°, not the 29.4° listed in Table 2. ## Verification Summary

The prescription was independently re-entered and checked by paraxial y–ν ray trace and an ABCD matrix trace.

| Quantity | Patent | Recomputed |
|---|---:|---:|
| EFL, wide | 16.50 mm | 16.4968 mm |
| EFL, middle | 33.99 mm | 33.9886 mm |
| EFL, tele | 53.40 mm | 53.4075 mm |
| BFD, wide | 17.92 mm | 17.9247 mm |
| BFD, middle | 17.92 mm | 17.9208 mm |
| BFD, tele | 17.92 mm | 17.9337 mm |

Group focal lengths in air are: G1 +96.57 mm, G2 -17.73 mm, G3 +21.46 mm, G4 -33.61 mm, and G5 +98.32 mm. The signs reproduce the +/−/+/−/+ architecture. Cemented subassembly checks give L11–L12 ≈ −7766 mm, L22–L23 ≈ +61.1 mm, L32–L33 ≈ −94.8 mm, L34–L35 ≈ −82.4 mm, and L41–L42 ≈ −33.6 mm.

The Petzval sum computed surface by surface as Σφ/(n·n′) is +0.003055 mm⁻¹, giving a Petzval radius of approximately −327 mm and P·fw ≈ 0.050. Semi-diameters were checked for edge thickness, surface slope, and cross-gap sag intrusion at all zoom positions; the closest air-gap constraints remain below the 90% intrusion threshold used by the data specification.
